# rysta board

stand-alone description of the Rysta board. covers the physical board only;
how it is used in any specific product lives elsewhere.

## summary

- existing module
- ESP8266EX MCU with 4 MB flash
- on-board LDO 5V -> 3.3V
- on-board button with edge-trigger wake circuit to `RST`
- on-board RGB LED
- breakout connector X3 for power and signals

## MCU

- ESP8266EX
- 4 MB flash
- internal pull-ups available on GPIOs except `GPIO 16`

## power

- input rail: +5V on connectors X2, X3 and micro-USB
- on-board LDO from 5V to 3.3V
- LDO drop ~100 mV at ~100 mA
- idle current via LDO ~80 µA in deep sleep at 3.7V input


## on-board button and wake circuit

- single momentary button on the board
- button connects to `GPIO 16`, pull-up 10 k
- `GPIO 16` is *not* directly connected to `RST`
- dedicated edge-trigger circuit converts a low transition on `GPIO 16`
	into a short negative pulse on `RST`
- consequence: every button press resets the MCU

### x3 wake and rst lines

both `WAKE` (`GPIO 16`) and `RST` are routed to X3 and are available
bidirectionally:

- as **output**: external circuitry can observe the on-board signal
	- `WAKE` mirrors the button state
	- `RST` mirrors the reset pulse generated by the wake circuit
- as **input**: external circuitry can pull the line low
	- pulling `WAKE` low triggers the wake circuit -> `RST` pulse
	- pulling `RST` low resets the MCU directly
- both lines are active-low and behave like wired-or inputs; any external
	low source has the same effect as the on-board source

## on-board rgb led

- common cathode, active high, driven directly from GPIOs
- pin mapping is listed in the connectors table

## boot-strap behavior

board-enforced boot levels are listed in the connectors table. chip-level
requirements are met by the board.

- `GPIO 16` driven high by on-board pull-up 10 k

ESP8266-level effects on un-driven pins (not specific to this board):

- uart0 txd emits boot-loader output at boot
- uart0 rxd is driven high at boot
